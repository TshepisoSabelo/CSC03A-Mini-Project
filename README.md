## Proposal Topic:
## Maize Disease Detection
### Motivation:
Maize is a key economic driver in South Africa, with a total production of approximately 14.5 million metric tons. The gross income from maize increased by 37.9%, reaching R64,826 million in 2022, up from R46,994 million in 2021.
Maize is a valuable crop in South Africa, but it is affected by numerous diseases such as Gray Leaf Spot, Maize Streak Virus, Common Rust, and others. These diseases impact maize in various ways, including stunted plant growth and reduced quality (poor nutrient transport leads to poorly filled kernels), ultimately resulting in a decrease in the amount of safe-to-eat produce.
This poses a significant problem in South Africa, where maize is a staple food. Low yields lead to reduced profitability and financial strain, threatening the sustainability of farming operations and national food security.
A potential solution is to implement a Maize Disease Detection System. This system would allow users to take pictures of crops they suspect are unhealthy. The images will be converted into graph representations, which will be used for analysis. The system would first determine whether the image is of a maize leaf by detecting similarities between the input image and images of maize leaf . If confirmed, it will then classify the type of disease present in the leaf . This system will enable users to detect diseases early and identify appropriate treatment methods.
